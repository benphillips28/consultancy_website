Our Services & Experience

1. Ecological Services

We offer support with ecological research - be it surveys or experiments - across both terrestrial and marine ecosystems. We are also able to provide practical management guidance.

Our experience covers...

  • Ecological studies - We can design and run surveys and experiments to help answer your ecological questions.
  • Habitat and land management – Including topics such as road verges, public green spaces, farmland, and pollinator habitats.
  • Marine conservation – Including topics such as marine megafauna, fisheries, and the impacts of microplastics.

2. Social Science Approaches

By revealing people's attitudes, motivations and behaviours, we can help you to shape more effective and inclusive strategies - in nature conservation and beyond.

Our experience covers a variety of social science approaches, including...

  • Questionnaire design - Including developing surveys of 18,000 adults across England with Natural England, and of people's attitudes and behaviours relating to pollinators.
  • Stakeholder interviews & focus groups - Including to guide development of a conservation strategy for hazel dormouse.
  • Analysis of digital data sources - Including combining large-scale survey, social media and digital data sources relating to people-nature relations with Natural England.

3. Evidence Synthesis

We are experienced in synthesising evidence to a rigorous scientific standard, helping you to make informed decisions based on the best available knowledge.

Our work spans a wide variety of ecological and environmental topics, with experience reviewing and summarising evidence on issues spanning...

  • Enhancing road verges for pollinators (with the conservation charity Buglife), and for broader ecosystem services (e.g. air quality, climate regulation, aesthetics).
  • Environmental links between forests and oceans for The Woodland Trust.
  • Plastic pollution and marine turtle entanglements.

4. Data Services

We can assist you with managing, visualising and analysing ecological and social data.

This includes spatial mapping and analysis using Geographical Information Systems (GIS), and projects involving large, unwieldy datasets.

Our experience includes...

  • Modelling road pollution across Great Britain.
  • Remotely assessing road verge habitats using Google Earth and Street View.
  • Comparing light pollution levels across UK local authorities and habitats.

5. Programme development

We can provide you with scientific support across all stages of grant writing and programme development - from shaping initial ideas to delivering robust, fundable proposals and implementing successful, well-managed projects.

Our experience includes...

  • Grant writing – Drawing on our experience as both applicants and reviewers, we can help craft compelling, impactful proposals that stand out.
  • Project management – Supporting the design, coordination and delivery of scientific research programmes and related activities.
  • Capacity building & community engagement – Including work on gender, equality and inclusive participation in environmental initiatives.
