2. Social Science Approaches

We are experienced in researching people's attitudes, values and behaviours, using surveys, interviews and digital data sources. This information can help to shape more effective and inclusive strategies in nature conservation and beyond.

Our experience includes...

  • Questionnaire design, including developing a survey of 18,000 adults across England with Natural England, and an online survey of public attitudes and behaviours relating to pollinators.
  • Analysis of large-scale survey, social media and digital data sources relating to people-nature relations with Natural England.
  • Conducting stakeholder interviews and focus groups to guide a conservation strategy for hazel dormouse.

3. Evidence Synthesis

We are experienced in synthesising evidence to a rigorous scientific standard, helping organisations to make informed decisions based on the best available knowledge.

Our work spans a wide range of ecological and environmental topics, with experience reviewing and summarising evidence on issues including...

  • Enhancing road verges for pollinators (with the conservation charity Buglife), and for broader ecosystem services (e.g. air quality, climate regulation, aesthetics).

4. Data Services

We can assist with managing, visualising and analysing ecological and social data. This includes spatial mapping and analysis using Geographical Information Systems (GIS), and projects involving large, unwieldy datasets.

Our experience includes...

  • Modelling road pollution across Great Britain.
  • Remotely assessing road verge habitats using Google Earth and Street View.
  • Comparing light pollution levels across UK local authorities and habitats.

Our experience includes...
